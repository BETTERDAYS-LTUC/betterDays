Addiction Recovery Timeline

Admitting You Have A Problem

Many individuals end up admitting that they have a problem after a terrible experience occurs, such as losing a relationship or job or an accident after a night out. Regardless of the experience that leads a person to this step, it is important to remember that, despite the fact that hitting bottom is a horrible feeling, things can only get better from here on out. It is both a sobering and empowering situation to be in.

Step One

Exploring Treatment Options

Once a person decides to make a change, it is time to start looking for drug recovery options, such as a drug rehab centre. Options for recovery include an inpatient or outpatient drug rehab centre. An inpatient drug addiction rehab centre involves living at the treatment facility that they will receive treatment.

Step two

Finding Support

Regardless of the treatment programme that a person chooses, they will need support and help throughout their journey. It is important to speak with family members and friends about their decision to enter a treatment programme and ask them for support.

Step Three

Making Sobriety Last

Achieving sobriety and completing a rehab programme are some of the first major steps in the drug recovery process. However, once a person has completed a programme, they still have to continue to maintain their sobriety and avoid relapse.

Step four

Keeping Hope Alive

The final step of the drug recovery process is for a person to forgive himself or herself if they relapse and not to let it interfere with their journey. Relapses provide a learning experience, even though they can be frustrating at the time.
